The Josephus Problem
Problem Definition:
The Josephus problem is defined as follows. Suppose that there are n people arranged in a circle and that we are given a positive integer m â‰¤ n . Beginning with a designated first person, we proceed around the circle, removing every mth person. After removing each person, counting continues around the circle that remains. This process continues until all n people have been removed. The order in which the people are removed from the circle defines the (n, m)-Josephus permutation of the integers 1, 2, ..., n. For example, the (7, 3) Josephus permutation is (3, 6, 2, 7, 5, 1, 4).
